Stormfield Cabin

Welcome to Stormfield Cabin

What's this all about?

We've got a little bit of Scotland just for us, our friends and our family - you can see the photos to get an idea of what it's like and here's the plan of the site. It's got 2 bedrooms, a stove, a smattering of midges and an awesome view. Thank you to everyone who helped make it happen.

How much does it cost?

It's free! Genuinely free. Honestly free. We want our friends and family to enjoy it and for it to be as occupied as much as possible throughout the year. So please, we'd love you to book in and head up there. If you'd like to contribute to electricity/firewood costs you can transfer us money - we think £5 per day covers it.

When can I go?

You can go whenever it's free! Or, if you don't mind sharing, maybe even when it's already booked! Find a date in the calendar and drop Jono & Ruth an email to book in.

What should I take with me?

It's a bit like a hotel room - really you just need yourself, your clothes, some food and a towel - everything else is taken care of. Here's what's there at the moment:

  • A 2 bedroom (one king, one double) cabin with a stove, hot shower, small kitchen and great views
  • Wifi
  • All your usual kitchen stuff - cups, plates, bowls, spoons, pans, hob, fridge, bottle opener, can opener, sharp knives, cutlery, etc
  • Fire wood (and an axe and saw in case you're feeling energetic)
  • Bedding and sheets
  • Power
  • A tumble dryer (that sometimes needs a few tries to get it to start)
  • Some books
  • A sofa
  • Many midges and some ticks
  • Great views

What do I do when I arrive?

  • Head out to the outside rear cupboard to turn on the water - the cupboard key looks like a really big T shape and is stored in the kitchen by the microwave. Turn on the mains water - unfortunately we have had two burst pipes so we're being extra cautious about leaving water turned off at the cabin in case anything happens again whilst we're away. To turn on the water there's tap inside the back cupboard. It's the brass tap, NOT THE RED TAP. Turn the brass tap two full turns to the left. (There's also a blue mains tap in a black box in the ground at the road side of the shed - this needs to turn from perpendicular to parallel with the pipe. During the summer we'll leave this turned on). The taps in the bathroom and kitchen are needing to be fixed by the plumber but do work at a lower pressure than you'd expect - patience needed. Sorry.
  • The back cupboard has a switch to give power to the bathroom tap and the kitchen tap and shower power are both under the kitchen sink
  • Pop on a wash and dry with the last people's sheets
  • Turn on the underfloor heating. The underfloor heating control is a small white box under the sink - turn this on. 17.5 degrees and leaving it on overnight suits us - it’s designed to be on all the time so feel free to fiddle to find optimal comfort for you!
  • Make yourself a cuppa!

Do you have any tips to get the best out of the stove?

The stove works great - here are some tips to really get it working well for you:

  • Leave the door open a little when you first light it
  • Don't worry about treating it like an open fire and leaving the door open all the time
  • There's a vent at the front below the door - pull it out to let air in and increase draw
  • When you're lighting it stack in plenty of paper, kindling and a couple of fire lighters
  • When it's burning down get the log in nice and quickly
  • Kindling and logs are in the shed

What do I do when I depart?

  • Turn off the mains water - unfortunately we had a burst pipe in December 2022 so we're being extra cautious about leaving water turned off at the cabin in case anything happens again whilst we're away. To turn off the water there's brass tap inside the back cupboard - turn it all the way clockwise to the right to turn the water off. In winter there's also a blue tap in a black box in the ground at the road side of the shed - this needs to turn from parralel to perpendicular with the pipe.
  • Turn off the bathroom tap power in the back cupboard
  • Turn off the kitchen tap power under the kitchen sink
  • Turn the underfloor heating controller under the kitchen sink to frost setting
  • Empty the bins under the sink - bins in the left hand end of the shed as follows: blue = paper and cardboard, green = plastic, metal and containers, grey = general waste, and there’s glass recycling opposite the Midge Bite. Bin day is alternating Monday - if either bin is full then feel free to move it to the driveway outside the gate but don't worry if the bins aren't full. Make sure you pop a stone on top!
  • If the stove is even a little warm then please leave but if it’s cold then there’s a black ash bucket in the outside cupboard - pop the ashes there and then back into the cupboard and we'll deal with them later
  • Check stove door is locked
  • Take your sheets off the beds and leave them in the laundry bag for the next guests to wash
  • Close the back bedroom and bathroom windows
  • Turn off all lights and fans
  • Check bathroom fan is off
  • Check kitchen fan is off
  • Check fridge is empty
  • Check that you have not left any phone chargers (or anything else!)
  • Sweep floor and wipe surfaces
  • Let us know about any breakages/issues
  • Lock up and put the key back in the key safe
  • To prevent burst pipes over winter, there's now a way to drain the water system. Turn off the power to both heated taps and the shower. Turn off the mains water (brass tap). Open up both taps inside. Remove the washing machine hose. Open the washing machine tap. Using the wet-vac hoover (stored in the shed), attach it to the red tap pipe and turn on to suck. You should get around a litre of water out.

What do I if there's an emergency or I've got a question?

Call us! For water-related emergencies turn the brass tap in the back cupboard clockwise and the water can be entirely turned off at both the black access panel on the ground outside by the shed and at the access panel across the road by the trees (mid-way between the pond and the Arthur Terrace houses).

The bathroom sink seems to airlock a lot. There's a plunger in the back cupboard and that seems to do the trick.

There's duck tape and a hoover in the shed.

Public transport

Public transport takes up to around 7 hours - it's a twenty second walk from the train station so public transport is a great option!

By train, the Edininburgh/Perth/Inverness/Achnasheen (2 changes) route takes 5 and a half hours or the Edinburgh/Inverness/Achnasheen (1 change) route takes around 6 and a half hours. Costs are variable but a mid-2019 rough calculation of 1 week in advance for 2 adults and 1 child was around £140 total for the return journey.

It's also possible to travel by bus in around 6 and a half hours using the Megabus M90 from Edinbrugh to Inverness, then Service 700 (Laide) by Westerbus. Cost estimate for 2 adults and 1 child was around £165 for the return journey.

What's the weather like?

Check out the Weather for the next 5 days (or just assume that there will be rain and midges!). Note that the MET office weather uses a weather station on a hilltop rather than at valley level so wind speeds/temperatures/etc on non-BBC weather forecasts might be a bit off.

Another thing to note is that it can get VERY windy at Achnasheen. The cabin has been "iterated on" a couple of times to make sure the window flashing didn't blow away, to add struts to hold the chimney and to add a porch to keep the rain out of the door. The cabin makes a fair bit of noise when it's stormy windy - best advice is just to try to ignore it and if anything does break or blow off let us know and we'll sort it out!

I found a tick on me - what do I do?

It seems that there are a lot of ticks at Achnasheen - ticks can cause you to be poorly so best removed quickly. There's a "tick key" and tweezers to remove ticks in the bathroom first aid kit (or the kitchen drawer). Advice seems to be to get it off as fast as possible and to keep an eye for rings appearing round the bite in the future. We've had several and not got ill (Jono's record was 13 at once!)

Waaaaaa - there's midges. What do I do?

There's Smidge midge repellent, midge nets and cotronella incense in the kitchen drawer. Smidge is pretty good and isn't filled with lots of bad chemicals. If you're in the cabin and it's hot but there are midges outside you can put the fan on the floor just by the door and the breeze should keep the midges away. The fan is in the back right cupboard. We're going to get a midge screen for the door. If it is sunny the cabin will get very hot in the afternoon - a good time to go exploring out and about - it will cool down fairly quickly into the evening.

Warning about the shed!

The inside ceiling of the shed is dangerous - please don't jump in it as there are lots of nails hanging down (as the internal insulation hasn't been added yet). It'll be finished soon... I promise!
